Grid 2.0 — Flipkart Hackathon

Python · CNN · Image Recognition · ROS

Top 1% among 20k+ teams in a multi-round robotics hackathon: CNN-based perception with ROS and real-time control for pick-and-place with a robotic arm.

Grid 2.0 was a Flipkart-sponsored hackathon focused on autonomous manipulation: teams progressed through several elimination rounds, and our entry finished in the top 1% out of more than twenty thousand.

I worked on the perception and execution stack in Python. We used a convolutional network for object detection and classification on camera frames, exposed results through ROS so planning nodes could subscribe to stable object poses in the robot frame.

On the robot side, RGB-D sensors fed the detector; arm motion was planned using that feedback so grasps stayed aligned as the scene changed. The emphasis was on low-latency inference and closed-loop corrections rather than a single open-loop plan—so the arm could adjust as detection noise and lighting varied.
