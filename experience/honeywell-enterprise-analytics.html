Honeywell · Backend Engineer · Jul 2022 to Nov 2024

Enterprise Analytics

Snowflake · Data lake · Salesforce · Azure · AWS · Prometheus · Fuzzy matching

I owned end to end data workflows for two high impact programs: a maintenance analytics stack that harmonized more than eighty Salesforce tables into a lake backed model for stakeholders, and a multi cloud cost intelligence pipeline that fused billing APIs with operational telemetry for spend optimization. I also shipped fuzzy entity resolution so customer and asset identities lined up consistently across SAP, Salesforce, and internal stores.

Across both programs the pattern was the same in the abstract: many heterogeneous sources, a governed landing and modeling zone, then curated outputs tuned for decisions: not a single straight line, but fan in, parallel refinement, and fan out to the right consumers.

Maintenance stakeholder program

Salesforce heavy operational truth, modeled for asset, site, and work order analytics in the data lake.

Reliability and maintenance leaders needed a single place to reason about work orders, assets, parts, service contracts, and account context without clicking through dozens of Salesforce objects. I designed a data workflow that extracted and loaded relational data from more than eighty Salesforce tables (object families spanning cases, installed assets, inventory, and related dimensions) into the data lake with incremental patterns suited to large, evolving schemas.

Preprocessing focused on consistency: keys that align entities across objects, handling of slowly changing attributes, null and duplicate policies, and joins that respect Salesforce’s implicit relationships once data left the UI. Post processing layered business ready aggregates and KPIs: cost and effort by machine, line, and site, SLA oriented views, and slices that made the biggest maintenance spend drivers obvious. That end to end model fed dashboards and downstream consumers so stakeholders saw insightful, decision grade data rather than raw extracts.

The diagram is intentionally nonlinear: the lake is a shared hub, preprocessing runs as parallel workstreams (keys and joins, quality and slowly changing history, reference hierarchies) before the model merges them, and post processing feeds several channels at once. That structure kept schema churn in Salesforce from breaking one fragile chain and let us extend new objects without rewiring the entire path. Stakeholder visibility into cost and downtime drivers contributed to roughly a 15% reduction in avoidable maintenance expense by focusing interventions where the data said they mattered.

Cloud cost monitoring & spend optimization

Billing APIs plus operational context from Azure Resource Graph and Prometheus style utilization signals.

Finance and platform teams needed more than invoice exports: they needed why spend moved and what to right size or retire. I built a pipeline that pulled cost and usage from the Azure Cost Management API and AWS cost APIs, then enriched that spine with operational metadata from Azure Resource Graph (resource topology, tags, SKUs, ownership) and utilization and health signals sourced from Prometheus backed dashboards so utilization was comparable to cost in the same analytical grain.

Normalization aligned currencies, billing periods, and account structures across clouds before joins. The combined layer supported spend optimization insights: underused clusters, orphaned disks, mismatched reservation coverage, and slices that tied technical owners to dollars. Outputs were tuned for both executive rollups and engineering drill downs.

Here the nonlinearity is the multi source ingest plus an explicit enrichment stage: after a unified spend core, Resource Graph attributes and Prometheus backed utilization series merge on shared resource keys so dollars and usage stay comparable. That is what makes “optimization” defensible. Tracked environments saw roughly a 20% reduction in relevant cloud spend once waste and misprovisioning were visible early.

Entity resolution (cross-system)

Fuzzy matching across SAP, Salesforce, and internal databases.

Customer and asset identity stayed fragmented across SAP, Salesforce, and custom databases. I implemented a fuzzy matching pipeline for entity resolution (blocking keys, tuned similarity thresholds, and human review queues for edge cases) that improved mapping accuracy on key datasets by approximately 35%, which downstream reporting and both programs above depended on for consistent rollups.
